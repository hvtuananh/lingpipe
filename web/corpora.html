This page contains links to linguistic corpora organized by language and type of annotation.

Named Entity Chunked

Arabic, Chinese, English: ACE 2004 Multilingual

Arabic, Chinese and English news data annotated for entities and relations. (Corpus includes English, Chinese and Arabic.)

LDC: ACE 2004

Arabic, Chinese, English: ACE 2005 Multilingual

Arabic, Chinese and English data from news, broadcast news, and broadcast conversation, annotated for entities, relations and within-document coreference.

LDC: ACE 2005

Chinese: SIGHan 3

The SIGHan group of ACL released three different Chinese corpora annotated for named entity: City University of Hong Kong (Big 5), Microsoft Research (CP936/unicode) and LDC (CP936/unicode). Also included word segmentation data as part of the distribution.

SIGHan: SIGHan 3

Chinese: Short Message Data Collation II

2.6M characters from 202K short "daily life" messages, with named entities. Related ELRA data sets also annotate Pinyin. There was also a 5.9M character collection Data Collation I.

ELRA: Chinese Short Message Data Collation

Dutch: CoNLL 2002

Dutch newswire annotated for standard named entities, person, location and organization, as well as part-of-speech.

CoNLL: CoNLL 2002 Dutch Corpus

English: TERN 2004

English newswire and broadcast news annotated for times.

LDC: TERN 2004

English: TimeBank 1.2

183 news articles annotated for temporal expressions and reference using TimeML.

LDC: TimeBank 1.2

English: CoNLL 2003

English newswire annotated for standard named entities.

CoNLL: CoNLL 2003 English

English: Message Understanding Conference (MUC) 6

318 Wall Street Journal articles annotated for person, location and organization entities. There is also an additional MUC 6 data.

LDC: MUC 6

English: Message Understanding Conference (MUC) 7

Newswire data tagged with named entity types (PERSON, LOCATION, ORGANIZATION), as well as times (DATE, TIME), and numbers (MONEY, PERCENT). There is an online tagging guide.

LDC MUC 7 Corpus

English: CoNLL 2003

English newswire annotated for standard named entities and part of speech.

CoNLL: CoNLL 2003 English

French: ESTER

60 hours of orthographically transcribed news broadcast, including annotations of named entities (person, place, data, organisation, geo-political entity, amount, building and unknown).

ELRA: ESTER Corpus

French: ARCADE II

316K words of French with named entities. Also includes alignment with Arabic named entities.

ELRA: Evaluation only license.

German: CoNLL 2003

German newswire annotated for standard named entities, part of speech, and lemmas.

CoNLL: CoNLL 2003 German

Hungarian: Szeged Named Entity

200K tokens of data from MTI annotated for person, location, organization and miscellaneous entities in CoNLL format. With semi-automatic part-of-speech tags.

Szeged HLT: Szeged NE

Italian: I-CAB

180K tokens from L'Adige, a local newspaper, annotated for ACE-style entities (person, location, organization, geo-political entity), and time. Also annotated for part of speech. In CoNLL format.

OntoText: Italian Content Annotation Bank

Spanish: CoNLL 2002

Spanish newswire annotated for standard named entities, person, location and organization, as well as part-of-speech.

CoNLL: CoNLL 2002 Dutch Corpus

Part-of-Speech Tagged

Also see the section on parsed data, as almost all parsed data also includes part-of-speech tags.

Arabic: NEMLAR Written Corpus

500K words of Arabic from a range of sources from the late 1990s to 2005. It contains fully vowel-inserted text, lexical/morphological analysis and part-of-speech tags.

ELRA: NEMLAR Written Corpus

English: British National Corpus

100M words of text with automatically generated POS tags with headings, paragraphs, sentences, etc. A broad range of sources including spoken language.

U. Oxford: British National Corpus

Chinese: Tagged Gigaword

A version of the Chinese gigaword corpus which was tagged for part-of-speech by Academia Sinica. It contains 1.3M words drawn from newspaper sources.

LDC: Tagged Chinese Gigaword

Dutch: PAROLE Corpus

250K words from news papers, transcribed TV news, books, and periodicals, automatically POS tagged with 60K checked by hand twice, 200K checked by hand once.

ELRA: Dutch PAROLE Corpus

Dutch: CoNLL 2002

Dutch newswire annotated for standard named entities, person, location and organization, as well as part-of-speech.

CoNLL: CoNLL 2002 Dutch Corpus

English: CoNLL 2000

Sections 15-18, and 20 of the Penn English Treebank (about 250K tokens), annotated for part-of-speech and noun-phrase chunking.

CoNLL: CoNLL 2000 Corpus; UPenn: Treebank Chunking Data

English: American National Corpus, Second Release

20M words of English from various sources. Annotated for morphology and part-of-speech (presumably automatically).

LDC: American National Corpus

English: Brown Corpus

1M words from a large range of sources annotated for part of speech using a fine-grained tagging of 100+ tags.

NLTK: NLTK Brown Corpus

English: MULTEXT JOC Corpus

200K words from teh Written Questions and Answers of the Official Journal of the European Community. Aligned at sentence level with French, Italian, and Spanish.

ELRA: MULTEX JOC Corpus

English: Wolverhampton Business English

10M words from 23 web sites relating to business. Converted to SGML and annotated for paragraph, sentence, and part-of-speech.

ELRA: Wolverhamptom Business English Corpus

English: CRATER 2 Corpus

International Telecommunications Union corpus of 1.5M tokens. These are aligned with French and Spanish CRATER data.

ELRA: CRATER 2 Corpus

French: CRATER 2 Corpus

International Telecommunications Union corpus of 1.5M tokens of French. Aligned with Spanish and English CRATER corpora.

ELRA: CRATER 2 Corpus

French: PAROLE Corpus

250K tokens from newspaper Le Monde (1987), automatically tagged. Also includes some classification features over 14M tokens of Le Monde, as well as some book and periodocal data.(?).

ELRA: French PAROLE Corpus

French: MULTEXT JOC Corpus

200K words from teh Written Questions and Answers of the Official Journal of the European Community. Aligned at sentence level with English, Italian, and Spanish.

ELRA: MULTEX JOC Corpus

French: Scientific Corpus

450K tokens drawn from La Recherche magazine, and annotated for part-of-speech and stem.

ELRA: French Scientific Corpus

German: MTP AnnotatedCorpus

500K words from newspapers Die Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung and Die Zeit (1990, 1992) over all topics. Sentence annotated and tag annotated with a coarse (52 tags) and fine (137 tags) tag set.

ELRA: MTP Annotated German Corpus

Greek: ILSP/ELEFTHEROTYPIA Corpus

3M words of Greek with documentws classified by PAROLE standard (medium, topic and genre), from the newspaper Eleftherotypia. 250K words annotated for part of speech and morphology. Alle checked by hand.

ELRA: ILSP/ELEFTHEROTYPIA Corpus

Italian: PAROLE Corpus

3M words from various newspapers, periodicals, books and documents from 1970 to 1996. The data is morphosyntactically annotated and lemmatized.

ELRA: Italian PAROLE Corpus

Italian: MULTEXT JOC Corpus

200K words from teh Written Questions and Answers of the Official Journal of the European Community. Aligned at sentence level with English, French, and Spanish.

ELRA: MULTEX JOC Corpus

Korean: Qualified POS

1M tokens (eojeols) morphologically analyzed and POS tagged. Triple-checked by hand.

ELRA: Korean Qualified POS

Korean: Morphologically Annotated

41K words of Korean with part of speech and morphological tags.

LDC: Morphologically Annotated Korean Text

Portuguese: PAROLE Corpus

250K words of Portuguese from newspapers, books, periodicals and other sources, annotated for morpho-syntactic relations. Manually checked.

ELRA: PAROLE Portuguese Corpus

Spanish: CoNLL 2002

Spanish newswire annotated for standard named entities, person, location and organization, as well as part-of-speech.

CoNLL: CoNLL 2002 Spanish Corpus

Spanish: MULTEXT JOC Corpus

200K words from teh Written Questions and Answers of the Official Journal of the European Community. Aligned at sentence level with English, French, and Italian.

ELRA: MULTEX JOC Corpus

Spanish: CRATER 2 Corpus

International Telecommunications Union corpus of 1.0M tokens of Spanish. Includes stems and morphological features. These are aligned with French and English CRATER data.

ELRA: CRATER 2 Corpus

Swedish: Stockholm Umeå Corpus

1M words of Swedish with balanced genres. Free for research. Don't know about commercial licenses.

Dept of Linguistics, Stockholm University: Stockholm Umeå Corpus

Urdu: EMILLE Lancaster Corpus

200K words of Urdu annotated for part-of-speech. Also includes other languages, and parallel texts.

ELRA: EMILLE Lancaster Corpus

Parsed

Arabic: Treebank Part 1 v 3.0

125K tokens of Arabic from AFT news in 2000. There is a treebank and morphosyntactic (part of speech) tags, including full vocalization and case endings.

There are also parts 2 and 3 available separately with similar markup.

LDC: Arabic Treebank Part 1 v 3.0

Chinese: Treebank 5.0

507K words annotated for sentence and part-of-speech tags (GB encoding).

LDC: Chinese Treebank 5.0

Czech: Prague Dependency Treebank 2.0

2M words of morphologically/POS annotated text, 1.5M words of syntactic annotation, and 0.8M words of semantic annotations.

LDC: Prague Dependency Treebank

English: Treebank-3

Includes part-of-speech tagged and parsed data for 1M tokens of newspaper text from 1989 Wall Street Journal and 1M tokens of Brown Corpus. Also includes tagged, parsed and disfluency-annotated Switchboard corpus of spoken dialog.

LDC: Treebank-3

English: ICE-GB

1M words making up the British (GB) component of the International Corpus of English (ICE). 1M words of spoken and written English. 84K parse trees, 60K of which are for spoken language. Fully hand checked.

ELRA: ICE-GB

English: BLLIP WSJ

Automatic annotation of 30M words of Wall Street Journal based on Treebank models (Charniak et al.).

LDC: BLLIP WSJ

English: CCGBank

Semi-automatic conversion of the English treebank into combinatory categorial grammar notation.

LDC: CCGBank

French: French Treebank

A French treebank of one million words tagged with morphosyntax and lemmas, compounds, phrasal lexical entries and phrase boundaries.

Anne Abeillé: French Treebank

German: NEGRA Corpus

Newspaper text from Frankfurter Rundschau, annotated with phrase-structure-like dependencies and part-of-speech tagged (convertible to Penn treebank format). Also includes 60K tokens with morphological analysis.

U. Saarland: NEGRA Corpus

German: Tiger Corpus

Newspaper text from Frankfurter Rundschau semi-automatically POS tagged and annotated for syntax. Morphological and lemma information on tokens. 900,000 tokens in 50,000 sentences. Distributed in NEGRA format or XML format. Also 2000 sentences annotated for dependencies.

U. Stuttgart: Tiger Corpus

Hungarian: Szeged Treebank

The Szeged corpus is 1.4M tokens of Hungarian in a range of topics (including news, fiction, law and student writing) annotated for part-of-speech, morphology, shallow syntax and semantics.

Szeged HLT: Szeged Treebank

Italian: Venice Italian Treebank

The VIT contains 272K tokens in six general domains, plus spoken language. There were 102 POS tags and 29 constituent tags in the fine analysis, 10 constituents and 22 POS tags in a coarse. Marked up like the Penn treebank in plain text.

ELRA: Venice Italian Treebank

Italian: Syntactic-Semantic Treebank

90K tokens financial news, 215K tokens general. Annotates both constituent/phrasal and grammatical relations, as well as sense tagging heads (vs. ItalWordNet).

ELRA: Italian Syntactic-Semantic Treebank

Korean: Treebank Annotations 2.0

Morphological and syntactic analysis of Korean newswire, using 647 articles from 2000.

Coreference Annotated

French: Modern French including Anaphors

1M words of books, scientific articles, newspapers and booklets. Anaphoric pronouns of all forms are marked. Delivered in XML with section, paragraph and sentence marking.

ELRA: Modern French including Anaphors
